# The Gilded Bazaar

## Product Principles & Engineering Handbook

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Living Document
**Last Updated:** July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the philosophy, principles and long-term vision of **The Gilded Bazaar**.

It is **not** a technical specification.

It exists to answer one question:

> **Why does this platform exist, and how should every future feature be designed?**

Every engineer, designer, AI coding agent and contributor should read this document before implementing any feature.

When technical decisions become difficult, this handbook takes precedence over convenience.

---

# Mission Statement

> **Reduce campaign administration to near zero while increasing immersion between sessions.**

The Gilded Bazaar exists to remove repetitive work from tabletop roleplaying games while enriching the shared campaign world.

The application should never distract from the tabletop experience.

Instead, it should make the time **between sessions** as engaging as the sessions themselves.

---

# Vision

The Gilded Bazaar will become the world's leading companion platform for tabletop roleplaying campaigns.

Rather than replacing existing character sheet applications or virtual tabletops, it will become the operating system that manages everything surrounding a campaign:

* Economy
* Merchants
* Downtime
* Campaign history
* AI-assisted content generation
* Notifications
* Shared world simulation
* Living settlements
* Party administration

The application should feel like a living extension of the campaign rather than another utility app.

---

# North Star

The platform should always strive to achieve the following objective:

> **A Dungeon Master can create an engaging town, economy and downtime experience in under five minutes.**

Every new feature should help move the platform closer to this goal.

---

# Product Pillars

## 1. Living Worlds

Campaigns should continue evolving even when nobody is playing.

Examples include:

* Seasonal changes
* Festivals
* Merchant restocking
* Economic shifts
* Political events
* Rumour generation
* Supply shortages
* Natural disasters
* Travelling merchants
* World history

Players should feel that time has passed when they return.

---

## 2. Meaningful Downtime

Downtime should become gameplay rather than bookkeeping.

Supported activities include:

* Crafting
* Business ownership
* Gambling
* Research
* Training
* Property management
* Mount care
* Hiring followers
* Trading
* Long-term magical projects

Downtime should reward planning and investment.

---

## 3. Effortless Administration

Every feature should reduce the amount of repetitive work required by the Dungeon Master.

Examples include:

* AI-generated merchants
* Purchase approvals
* Shared inventories
* Party feed
* Campaign logs
* Notifications
* Session summaries
* Economic calculations

The platform should automate administration while leaving creative decisions to the players.

# Product Principles

## Principle 1

The application is a **campaign companion**, not a replacement character sheet.

Existing character sheet platforms already solve that problem.

The Gilded Bazaar complements them.

---

## Principle 2

AI augments creativity.

AI should inspire ideas, reduce repetitive work and automate calculations.

It should never replace imagination.

---

## Principle 3

The Dungeon Master always has final authority.

Every AI-generated result is a suggestion.

Every automated action can be overridden.

---

## Principle 4

Every interaction should require as few taps as possible.

Simple actions should usually take no more than three taps.

---

## Principle 5

The application should remain useful without premium features.

Paid functionality should enhance convenience rather than restrict core gameplay.

---

## Principle 6

Structured data always comes before presentation.

AI should generate structured, validated data first.

User-facing text is produced afterwards.

---

## Principle 7

Offline capability is a first-class feature.

Players should be able to browse inventories, review campaign information and manage previously synchronised data without an internet connection.

---

## Principle 8

Every action should leave an audit trail.

Purchases, approvals, credits, invitations and moderation actions should be traceable.

---

## Principle 9

Consistency is more valuable than novelty.

Every screen should feel familiar.

Every interaction should follow predictable patterns.

---

## Principle 10

Performance is part of the user experience.

Animations should feel smooth.

Loading should be minimal.

The application should feel responsive even on older devices.

---

# Things We Will Never Build

The project intentionally excludes certain ideas.

## We will never:

* Replace full character sheet managers.
* Force players to use their phones during active gameplay.
* Hide essential gameplay behind payments.
* Allow AI to overrule the Dungeon Master.
* Require a permanent internet connection for core functionality.
* Generate copyrighted rules content that users are not licensed to access.
* Build features simply because they are technically interesting.

Every feature must solve a genuine problem.

---

# The Magic Test

Before implementing any feature, ask the following questions.

1. Does this reduce work for the Dungeon Master?
2. Does this increase immersion for the players?
3. Will someone smile the first time they use it?

A feature should satisfy at least two of these criteria before being approved.

---

# Engineering Principles

Every implementation should follow these standards.

* Screens contain presentation logic only.
* Business logic belongs in services.
* AI prompts are version-controlled.
* Every API is typed.
* Every database table uses Row Level Security.
* Every purchase is verified server-side.
* Every premium entitlement is validated by the backend.
* Every state transition is auditable.
* Every important feature is documented before implementation.
* Every specification becomes part of the Source of Truth.

# Design Principles

The user interface should communicate quality without unnecessary complexity.

### Themes

* Deep charcoal foundations
* Rich parchment surfaces
* Metallic gold highlights
* Emerald success states
* Crimson danger states
* Arcane blue magical accents

### Motion

Animations should feel:

* Calm
* Deliberate
* Magical
* Premium

Avoid:

* Cartoon physics
* Excessive bouncing
* Flashy transitions

### Typography

Use typography to reinforce immersion.

Large headings.

Readable body text.

Fantasy flavour through subtle accents rather than decorative overload.

---

# AI Philosophy

Artificial intelligence should remain largely invisible.

The player experiences:

* Better merchants
* Richer worlds
* Smarter notifications
* Better generated content

They should not feel like they are "talking to an AI."

The AI powers the world behind the scenes.

---

# Long-Term Vision

The Gilded Bazaar should evolve into a platform capable of supporting multiple tabletop roleplaying systems through configurable data rather than hard-coded mechanics.

The platform layer manages:

* Accounts
* Campaigns
* Merchants
* Economy
* Inventories
* Notifications
* AI orchestration
* Downtime
* World simulation

Rules-specific content is implemented as modular data, enabling future support for additional game systems.

---

# Definition of Success

The project will be considered successful when:

* Dungeon Masters spend significantly less time on administration.
* Players actively engage with the campaign between sessions.
* Campaigns develop persistent histories.
* The application feels immersive without becoming intrusive.
* Every new feature reinforces the product vision.
